Background

Big X, also known as Xavier Landum, had a life that was anything but ordinary before his rise to fame as a rapper. Growing up in Dallas, Texas, he faced adversity from a young age, witnessing his mother defend their home with a gun during a robbery when he was just a child. This traumatic experience, along with a challenging upbringing, shaped his tough exterior and street-smart mentality. His athletic prowess earned him a football scholarship to Crown College in Minnesota, but his time there was cut short due to legal troubles stemming from drug dealing. It was during his incarceration that he discovered his passion for music, using jail-issued medical forms to write his first raps. This newfound talent ignited a spark within him, setting the stage for his eventual transition into the world of hip-hop.

Culture and Collaboration


Big X The Plug worked to put his name on the map, but that wasn't the only thing rapping did for him. Much of his music describes how his fame and wealth have helped his family grow. His music was created for him and his people, with multiple instances of him asking for a verse so that other members of his group could make a name for himself. Similarly the song "Texas" was selected by his people, though Big X himself thought it needed work. Big X's music, though carrying a strong or boastful pace, has room for his generosity and love to come forward.
